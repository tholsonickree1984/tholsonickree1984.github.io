    Introduction

    As you must have already heard, the American roulette wheel has 38 divisions, where numbers from 1 to 36, 0 and 00 can be found. Numbers from 1 to 36 are alternately colored in black and red, while the single zero and the double zero are marked in green. The main objective of the player is to predict at which numbered pocket the roulette ball is going to settle into. To do this, all players place their bets on a particular number, after which the dealer rotates the roulette wheel in one direction and the roulette ball in the opposite direction. Once the ball settles into the pocket with that particular number, the respective players get paid. Roulette is primarily a game of luck and all 38 numbers are identically arranged on the wheel, thus, the ball has an equal probability to settle in any of the divisions.
