    How to Play Baccarat

    When playing Baccarat, there are just three bets that can be placed. Players will determine the amount of their bet and will then place it with the banker, player or as a tie bet. The banker and player bets will offer even money payouts, though there is a 5% commission on the banker bet. The tie bet offers a payout of 8:1, but it has a very high house edge. This bet has a house edge of 14%, so it is usually avoided by most players even though it can offer handsome payouts.

    Once bets are placed, the cards will be dealt. Players will not touch the cards and there are no choices that are made. The only choice in the game is where to place the bet. Two hands will be dealt on the table, one is the banker and the other is the player. The goal of the game is to bet on the hand that will have a value as close to 9 as possible. The hand values are determined by adding the value of the two cards and only using the last number. For example, an 8 and a 5 will be a 13 hand, so the value is actually 3. An Ace will be counted as one and all face cards have no value.

    In some cases, a third card will be used and this is determined by the dealer automatically. If the player hand has a value of 0-5, a card will be drawn. The Banker hand will draw based on what happens with the player hand. If the player hand is 1 or 2, the Banker will draw the third card. The third card rule can be quite complicated, but players at the table do not need to know these rules and the card will be dealt automatically when it is needed.
